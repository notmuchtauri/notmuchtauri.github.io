Standing on the Shoulders of Giants

Rather than reinventing the wheel and managing IMAP/SMTP protocols directly in Rust or JavaScript, NotmuchTauri acts as an orchestrator for four open-source pillars.

isync / mbsync

The sync engine. It reliably connects to your IMAP accounts and downloads your emails locally (Maildir format). This allows for a perfect offline-first experience.

Notmuch

The core reactor. A mail indexer and search engine based on Xapian. Stop wasting time sorting emails into folders: archive everything and find any message among hundreds of thousands in milliseconds using queries and tags.

msmtp

A minimalist and highly efficient SMTP client. When NotmuchTauri needs to send an email, it generates the message and pipes it directly to msmtp for dispatch.

LanguageTool

A powerful open-source grammar and spell checker. Running locally, it analyzes your drafts and suggests corrections without your data ever leaking to third-party servers.

Coding with Agentic AI: The Claude Code Experience

One of the most fun aspects of this project was the development approach. To refine the specs and structure complex Vue.js components (like recursive reply trees or placing overlays on text), I experimented with direct exchanges with Claude.

This "Specification-Driven Development" approach assisted by AI proved to be incredibly effective.

RagaRennes: AI directly in your inbox

As a computer scientist in 2026, I couldn't build a mail client without integrating AI to save time! I embedded RagaRennes (or any OpenAI-compatible API) as a collapsible side copilot.

  • Summarize endless threads: The UI converts complex HTML threads into structured plain text and sends it to the AI for a quick synthesis of the exchange.
  • Draft replies: Give it a few keywords ("Tell him the quote is validated and we start Monday"), and the AI generates the formatted Markdown text. One click injects it cleanly into the Tiptap editor.
